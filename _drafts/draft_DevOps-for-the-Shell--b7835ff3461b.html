DevOps for the Shell.

the lamentations of a release engineer’s dotfile experience. 

I recently finally decided to sit down and put together a real process together for managing my dotfiles. This is my story. 


Everyone writes their own dotfiles, and a bunch of people keep them in version control. But I wanted something more. than just dotfiles. 


I wanted to have a repo I could stick onto any computer I wanted, anywhere, forever. and have all of my tools and utilities available to me. This would be challenging to do. 

I basically wanted my dotfiles to be completely shell and machine agnostic and give me everything. 


This involved some shell hacking that got more and more complicated as time went on. 

What I didn’t realize was that I was basically writing what has already become Puppet. Puppet being for machine provisioning.
